# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A broad, mixed audience: individual contributors tracking personal work, teams coordinating projects and tasks, managers overseeing progress, and technical users integrating with other tools. Users open the product mid-workflow — in a meeting, between context switches, under deadline — so clarity and speed matter more than novelty.

## Product Purpose

A SaaS platform for collaborative project management and team communication. It helps teams track work, assign tasks, manage deadlines, and stay aligned through discussion — all in one place. Success looks like a team that misses fewer things, spends less time in status meetings, and trusts that shared context is up to date.

## Brand Personality

Warm, readable, human. The product should feel like a thoughtful colleague, not a corporate tool. Reference points: Notion's editorial warmth, Craft's attention to detail, Loom's approachable tone. Three-word personality: **grounded, clear, alive**.

Emotional goals: confidence (users trust the tool to hold their work), calm (the interface doesn't create anxiety), and a quiet sense of momentum (things are moving forward).

## Anti-references

- **Dark + neon/purple gradients** — the 2023–2025 startup dark-mode cliché. Feels like a crypto dashboard, not a work tool.
- **Glassmorphism / blur-heavy aesthetics** — decorative blur cards, frosted-glass everything. Feels unstable, not trustworthy.
- Generic SaaS cream/sand body backgrounds — warm-beige layouts that look AI-generated and brandless.
- Overly corporate blue-gray palette (Salesforce-style) — conservative to the point of lifeless.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm before clever** — reduce cognitive load at every decision. A user in the middle of their workday doesn't have attention to spare for clever interactions.
2. **Surface the work, not the tool** — the interface recedes; the content (tasks, conversations, progress) comes forward. Chrome should earn its presence.
3. **Warmth through detail** — personality lives in small moments: thoughtful microcopy, considered hover states, well-chosen weights. Not in decorative flourish.
4. **Trust through consistency** — repeating patterns build confidence. Users shouldn't have to re-learn the interface on different screens.
5. **Legible at speed** — hierarchy, contrast, and spacing tuned for scanning, not reading. A user 40 minutes into a sprint review should still be able to parse the screen instantly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations. Color must never be the sole carrier of meaning.
